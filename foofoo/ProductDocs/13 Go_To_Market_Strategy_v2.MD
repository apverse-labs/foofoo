# 1. Strategic Overview

Our go-to-market strategy is built on a core principle: **launch quietly, learn fast, scale with confidence.** Rather than a big-bang public launch, we follow a phased approach where each stage validates assumptions, collects data, and refines the product before expanding to the next.

The MVP is not a product launch — it’s a **market test**. We’re not trying to acquire thousands of users; we’re trying to answer one question: does this product solve a real problem that people will use daily? Everything — marketing, channels, messaging, spend — is calibrated to that question at each phase.

| **Phase** | **Goal** | **Platform** | **Monetization** | **Marketing** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **MVP** | Validate product relevance | Android only | None (all free) | Invite-only, organic |
| **Phase 1** | Full product + iOS + revenue | Android + iOS | Ads + Premium tiers | Organic + social + paid digital |
| **Phase 2** | Depth + premium features | Android + iOS | Full monetization | Digital marketing, ASO |
| **Phase 3** | Growth + social + partnerships | Android + iOS | All streams | Influencers, bloggers, B2B |

# 2. MVP Launch (Soft Launch — Test Market)

## 2.1 Objective

The MVP launch is **not a marketing event**. It is a controlled experiment to answer:

- Does the core concept work? Do people actually use an AI meal suggestion app daily?

- Is the swipe-based interaction intuitive? Do users engage with the Rubik’s Cube home page?

- What is the suggestion acceptance rate? Are the AI recommendations relevant?

- How does onboarding perform? Where do users drop off?

- What behavioral data can we collect to train and refine the Recommendation Engine?

## 2.2 Launch Parameters

| **Parameter** | **Details** |
| --- | --- |
| **Platform** | Android only (Google Play Store) |
| **Access Model** | Invite-only. Users need a referral code or direct invite link to download and register. |
| **Target User Count** | 100–300 users. Small enough to observe individually, large enough to spot patterns. |
| **Target Geography** | Mumbai, Bangalore, Pune — metros with high smartphone + food delivery penetration |
| **Duration** | 4–6 weeks of active testing before deciding to proceed to Phase 0.5 |
| **Monetization** | Zero. All features free. No ads. No premium gates. |
| **Marketing Spend** | ₹0. Purely organic, personal networks, and direct invites. |
| **Pricing Visibility** | Premium tier visible in UI but marked ₹0 ("Free during early access") |
| **Feedback Collection** | In-app feedback button, WhatsApp group for testers, weekly check-in surveys |

## 2.3 How We Get 100–300 Users (Zero Budget)

| **Source** | **Approach** | **Expected Users** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Personal Network** | Both co-founders invite friends, family, and extended network. Personal WhatsApp messages explaining the app and asking them to try it. | **50–80 users** |
| **Co-founder’s Colleagues** | Share with professional networks, office WhatsApp groups, LinkedIn connections who fit the target demographic. | **30–50 users** |
| **Friends of Friends** | Ask initial users to invite 2–3 people each. Referral reward (15 days premium) active from day one. | **30–70 users** |
| **Local Community Groups** | Apartment complex WhatsApp groups, society groups, gym/fitness community groups in target cities. | **20–50 users** |
| **College Alumni Networks** | Tap into alumni WhatsApp groups and LinkedIn networks. | **10–30 users** |

**Total expected: 140–280 users.** This is not about volume — it’s about getting the right users who will actually use the app daily and give honest feedback.

## 2.4 MVP Success Criteria (Go/No-Go for Phase 0.5)

| **Metric** | **Target** | **Why This Matters** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Daily Active Users (DAU)** | **>****30% of signups** | Are people coming back daily? If not, the core loop is broken. |
| **Suggestion Acceptance Rate** | **>****35%** | Are AI recommendations relevant enough to act on? |
| **Onboarding Completion** | **>****70%** | Is the 7-step onboarding too long or confusing? |
| **7-Day Retention** | **>****20%** | Do users find value beyond the first curiosity visit? |
| **Average Sessions per Week** | **>****4** | Are users checking the app for multiple meals? |
| **Swipe Engagement** | **>****5 swipes per session** | Is the Tinder-style mechanic working? |
| **Qualitative Feedback** | **Net positive** | Do users say "this is useful" or "I don’t need this"? |
| **Critical Bugs** | **<****3 blocking bugs** | Is the app stable enough to expand? |

**Decision rule:** If 5+ of 8 metrics hit targets, proceed to Phase 0.5. If 3–4 hit, iterate on MVP for 2–4 more weeks. If <3 hit, fundamental rethink required before investing further.

# 3. Phase 1 Launch (Full Product + iOS + Monetization)

## 3.1 Objective

Phase 1 is the **real public launch**. It runs across 4 sprints over 8 weeks (Weeks 15–22), immediately following the MVP soft launch. The app transitions from a validated test product to a full-featured business: iOS launches, monetization begins (ads + Premium tiers), and the feature set deepens significantly with recipes, pantry, guest mode, and context-aware AI explanations.

## 3.2 Phase 1 Feature Rollout (by Sprint)

| **Sprint** | **Features** | **GTM Impact** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Sprint 1 (Wk 15–16)** | Google Sign-In, Guest Mode, Context Tags, Extended Notifications, Carousel End → Search, Additional Profile fields | Removes signup friction (Google). Guest Mode lets curious users try without commitment. Context Tags ("Suggested because it’s rainy") build AI trust. All shipped via OTA — no app store update needed. |
| **Sprint 2 (Wk 17–18)** | Advanced Search Filters, Ingredient Comparison UI prep, iOS build + Apple App Store submission | Search unlocks active exploration beyond passive suggestions. iOS opens Apple ecosystem — critical for premium-willing demographics. 50 dishes now have full Level 3 recipes. |
| **Sprint 3 (Wk 19–20)** | Full Pantry system (item list, search add, photo add), AdMob ad integration | Pantry is the key differentiator — "check if you have ingredients" drives daily utility. Ad integration = first revenue. Both require app store updates. |
| **Sprint 4 (Wk 21–22)** | Recommendation pool (explore feed), video recipes (20 dishes), performance optimization, Phase 1 retrospective | Instagram-style discovery feed increases time-in-app. Video recipes are highly shareable content. 100 dishes now have full recipes. Database at 750 dishes. |

## 3.4 Phase 1 Target Metrics

| **Metric** | **Target** | **Rationale** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Total Downloads** | **5,000–15,000** | Organic + referrals + early paid campaigns |
| **Monthly Active Users** | **2,500–7,500** | 50% of downloads should be active monthly |

| **Recipe Engagement** | **>****20% of accepted suggestions view full recipe** | Users want to cook, not just browse |
| **Referral Rate** | **>****15%** | Each user refers at least one person on average |
| **7-Day Retention** | **>****25%** | Improved from MVP with notifications and deeper features |
| **App Store Rating** | **>****4.0 stars** | Critical for ASO and organic discovery |

**Go/No-Go for Phase 2:** DAU/MAU >30%, acceptance rate >40%, retention >25%, and premium conversion >3%. If metrics don’t hit thresholds, iterate before building forward — this is the rule from the Sprint Plan.

# 4. Phase 2 & Phase 3 Preview

## 4.1 Phase 2: Depth & Premium Features

Phase 2 is triggered when Phase 1 metrics are consistently hit for 2+ months. This phase adds the premium-exclusive features that drive subscription revenue and deepen engagement:

| **Feature** | **GTM Impact** |
| --- | --- |
| **Family Voting** | Family members vote on meals. Majority wins. Creates multi-user engagement within households. Premium feature. |
| **Cooking Diary** | "I Cooked" button logs meals with photos and feedback. Creates a personal food journal. Shareable content. |
| **Mood Selector** | Chill, Festive, Party, Comfort, Adventurous moods influence suggestions. Deepens AI personalization. |
| **User Modes** | Fasting, Recovery, Child-Friendly, Fitness modes with tailored meal plans. Premium feature. |
| **Meal Insights ****&**** Analytics** | Weekly/monthly eating patterns, cuisine variety, nutrition overview. Premium feature that drives conversions. |
| **Health Profiles** | Pregnancy, diabetes, recovery diets with validated nutrition guidance. Premium feature. |
| **Smart Ingredient Planning** | Auto-generated weekly grocery list from meal plan. Premium feature. |
| **User Ratings ****&**** Variants** | Rate dishes, submit recipe modifications. Community-contributed content begins. |

## 4.2 Phase 3: Growth, Social & Partnerships

Phase 3 introduces the social layer and partnership-driven growth. This is where marketing spend scales significantly. Detailed planning happens closer to execution:

| **Initiative** | **Approach** |
| --- | --- |
| **Food Blogger Partnerships** | Partner with 20–50 micro-influencers (10K–100K followers) for paid reviews, recipe collaborations, and "challenge" content (e.g., "I let an AI decide my meals for a week"). Budget: ₹50K–1L/month. |
| **Influencer Campaigns** | 2–3 larger influencers (100K+) per quarter for broader reach. Focus on lifestyle and mom/parenting influencers who match our core persona. |

# 9. GTM Risks & Mitigation

| **Risk** | **Impact** | **Mitigation** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **MVP users don’t engage daily** | Core loop might not be compelling enough | Iterate on suggestion quality and UX before Phase 0.5. Add push notifications earlier if needed. |
| **Organic social content doesn’t get traction** | Phase 1 growth stalls | Test different content formats aggressively. Study what food content goes viral in India. Pivot to WhatsApp-first if Instagram fails. |
| **Low premium conversion in Phase 1** | Revenue targets missed | A/B test pricing (₹49 vs ₹99). Add premium-exclusive features. Extend free trial. Consider ₹1 for first month. |
| **High CAC on paid channels** | Unsustainable unit economics | Pause paid, double down on organic + referral. Optimize targeting. Test different creatives. |
| **Negative app store reviews** | Kills organic discovery | Respond to every review. Fix bugs within 48 hours. Over-invest in stability. Ask happy users to rate. |
| **Referral gaming / abuse** | Premium given away without real growth | Cap at 6 months. Require referee to be active 7+ days. Track referral quality. |
